A Life of Service and Leadership

His Highness Alhaji Ahmad Nuhu Bamalli, CFR, ascended to the throne as the 19th Emir of Zazzau on October 7, 2020. A distinguished lawyer, diplomat, and public servant, his appointment marked the return of the Mallawa ruling house to the throne after nearly a century.

Born on June 8, 1966, in Zaria, His Highness earned his LLB from Ahmadu Bello University and later a Master's degree in International Affairs and Diplomacy. His professional career spans banking, public service, and diplomacy, having served as Nigeria's Ambassador to Thailand with concurrent accreditation to Myanmar prior to his appointment as Emir.

Throughout his reign, Emir Bamalli has focused on modernization while preserving the rich cultural heritage of Zazzau. His leadership emphasizes education, community development, and the promotion of peace and unity across the diverse communities of the emirate.

Zazzau Heritage Timeline

A journey through centuries of leadership, culture, and tradition

1

Origins of Zazzau

Circa 11th Century - Zazzau emerges as one of the original Hausa Bakwai city-states, founded by Queen Bakwa Turunku with its capital at Turunku.

2

Fulani Jihad & Islamic Influence

Early 19th Century - Zazzau becomes part of the Sokoto Caliphate following the Fulani Jihad, with Malam Musa Bamalli establishing the Mallawa dynasty.

3

Colonial Era

1903 - British forces capture Zaria, incorporating the emirate into the Northern Nigeria Protectorate while maintaining traditional structures.

4

Post-Independence

1960 onwards - Zazzau Emirate continues as a traditional institution within the Nigerian federal system, maintaining cultural authority and community leadership.

5

A New Era

October 7, 2020 - Alhaji Ahmad Nuhu Bamalli ascends to the throne as the 19th Emir of Zazzau, marking the return of the Mallawa ruling house after nearly a century.
