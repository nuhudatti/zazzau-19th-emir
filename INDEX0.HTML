His Highness Alhaji Ahmad Nuhu Bamalli, CFR, ascended to the throne as the 19th Emir of Zazzau on October 7, 2020. A distinguished lawyer, diplomat, and public servant, his appointment marked the return of the Mallawa ruling house to the throne after nearly a century.

Throughout his reign, Emir Bamalli has focused on modernization while preserving the rich cultural heritage of Zazzau. His leadership emphasizes education, community development, and the promotion of peace and unity across the diverse communities of the emirate.

Zazzau Heritage Timeline

A journey through centuries of leadership, culture, and tradition

Origins of Zazzau

Circa 11th Century

Zazzau emerges as one of the original Hausa Bakwai city-states, founded by Queen Bakwa Turunku with its capital at Turunku.

Fulani Jihad & Islamic Influence

Early 19th Century

Zazzau becomes part of the Sokoto Caliphate following the Fulani Jihad, with Malam Musa Bamalli establishing the Mallawa dynasty.

Colonial Era

1903

British forces capture Zaria, incorporating the emirate into the Northern Nigeria Protectorate while maintaining traditional structures.

Post-Independence

1960 onwards

Zazzau Emirate continues as a traditional institution within the Nigerian federal system, maintaining cultural authority and community leadership.

A New Era

October 7, 2020

Alhaji Ahmad Nuhu Bamalli ascends to the throne as the 19th Emir of Zazzau, marking the return of the Mallawa ruling house after nearly a century.
